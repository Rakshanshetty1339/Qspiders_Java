    Andaman Isalands


    Coorg, officially known as Kodagu, is a scenic district nestled in the Western Ghats of Karnataka, India. Often referred to as the "Scotland of India," it's a popular hill station renowned for its stunning landscapes, lush coffee plantations, misty hills, cascading waterfalls, and rich history and culture. Top attractions and things to do Abbey Falls: A beautiful waterfall nestzled amidst spice plantations, offering breathtaking views. Dubare Elephant Camp: Provides a unique opportunity to interact with elephants, including feeding and bathing them. Madikeri Fort: A 17th-century fort showcasing the region's history, housing a museum and offering insights into the town. Raja's Seat: A scenic viewpoint with gardens and musical fountains, ideal for enjoying
